Communism Alone

What particularly fascinates me about the comic is the notion that communism, at least as envisioned by the author, is alone. There are, of course, dastardly communists out there, but the dastardly communists are alone and separated from the rest of society. Their entire success is broken down to the conspiratorial motions of a few individuals.

There is not even an attempt to argue that the communists are developing a false consciousness 1 The notion that a group of people might develop goals contrary to their own interests. of sorts, but rather a complete denial that the communists could in fact be popular. Everything is done out of pure evil desire to make people suffer, and not particularly a desire to enrich themselves.

However, this position is a very safe position, both for the reader and for the author. If they tried to give the enemy comprehensible motivations then they would have to hope that their own counter-arguments would suffice. Secondly, they would have to understand that it's not alien monsters who create what they consider terrible, but rather people just the same as they are.
